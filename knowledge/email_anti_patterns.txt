# Cold Email Anti-Patterns — What NOT to Do
# These teach the Writer agent to avoid common mistakes.

## Anti-Pattern 1: The Generic Template
BAD:
Subject: Internship Opportunity
"Dear Hiring Manager, I am writing to express my interest in a software engineering
internship at your esteemed company. I am a passionate and hardworking individual..."

WHY IT'S BAD: No personalization, no specific reference to the person or company.
Could be sent to literally anyone. Goes straight to trash.

---

## Anti-Pattern 2: The Novel
BAD:
"Hi, let me tell you about my entire life story, every project I've ever built,
every course I've taken, my GPA, my extracurriculars, my volunteer work..."

WHY IT'S BAD: Too long. Nobody reads a 500-word cold email. Keep it under 120 words.
Respect their time.

---

## Anti-Pattern 3: The Buzzword Salad
BAD:
"I'm a passionate, results-driven, innovative self-starter with a growth mindset
who thrives in fast-paced environments and loves to leverage synergies..."

WHY IT'S BAD: Empty words that say nothing. Be specific about what you've done.

---

## Anti-Pattern 4: The Demand
BAD:
"I'd love to interview for a position on your team. When can we schedule a call?
Please find my resume attached."

WHY IT'S BAD: Too aggressive. You're asking a stranger for a favor — make a soft ask.
"Would you be open to a 15-min chat?" not "When can we schedule an interview?"

---

## Anti-Pattern 5: The Fake Flattery
BAD:
"I've been following your amazing career and I'm such a huge fan of everything
you've done. You're truly an inspiration to me."

WHY IT'S BAD: Feels disingenuous unless backed by something specific. Always reference
a SPECIFIC piece of their work, not vague admiration.

---

## Anti-Pattern 6: The Mass Blast
BAD:
"Hi [FIRST_NAME], I noticed you work at [COMPANY] as a [TITLE]..."

WHY IT'S BAD: Obvious mail merge. The personalization tokens are visible even when
filled in — the email still reads like a template. Weave the personal details
naturally into the message.

---

## Anti-Pattern 7: Missing CAN-SPAM Compliance
BAD: Any email without your real name, a way to opt out, or with a deceptive subject line.

WHY IT'S BAD: It's literally illegal (up to $53,088 per email). Always include:
- Your real full name
- A line like "If you'd rather not hear from me, feel free to let me know"
- Honest, non-clickbait subject line

---

## Key Rules for the Writer:
1. ALWAYS reference something SPECIFIC about the recipient
2. Keep under 150 words (120 ideal)
3. Soft ask only ("Would you be open to..." not "When can we...")
4. No buzzwords — concrete details only
5. Sound like a real person, not a template
6. Include CAN-SPAM footer (name + opt-out)
7. Subject line must be specific and honest
